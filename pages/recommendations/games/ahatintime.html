"You have all this power, and you're not gonna use it to fight evil?"

I had always liked the more easy-going platformers, especially if they're 3D. I've never been too fond of it if such games required stuff that mostly required good precise timing and movement. Luckily, A Hat in Time is nothing like that (at least for me), and fills my want for a fun 3D platformer completely.

At its core, it's just another of those platformer collectation games with bosses and puzzles. However, there is just something to this game that draws me to it more than games that are similar. It looks cute, the worlds are really varied, the music is great, the characters are good. And while the story is as simple as it can be, it's surprisingly well thought out. Every world has its own story to it, which you will figure out as you keep playing the game.

The thing that stood out to me most was the very diverse cast of characters. Each of them is memorable in their own ways due to their personalities and speech. While on the topic of speech, as far as I'm aware, everything in the game is voiced (if spoken by a character of course), and the voices are also just really good and fitting. Of course, the music is also well done, with each world having multiple tracks depending on the level you are doing. And naturally, each boss has its own theme too, generally following a leitmotif in typical fashion of boss battle themes.

If you are on the fence buying this game, just go for it. At this point I have completed the game a few times already and it has never bored me. And if you want to mix some things up after all, the game has some great DLCs, as well as custom content support, so there is always enough to do!
