“Hi! Lea! Hi!”

For the longest time, a friend kept pestering me to play this game as a joke, as it is one of his favorite games. While I did intend to play it for a while because of him, I kept postponing starting it, also as a joke in response. I really should have started it earlier though, as this game is genuinely fantastic.

The game plays in this pretend MMO world, so the gameplay feels somewhat like a typical MMORPG. Defeating enemies, finishing dungeons and (sometimes annoying) puzzles, leveling skills, and doing sidequests. There are quite a bunch of controls to remember, and during the game I have made quite some mistakes because I pressed the wrong button, however, that is fully a me problem, and it didn't take away the fun I had. That being said though, there is also quite a lot to think about. Enemies have elemental weaknesses, and some enemies have a better way to take them down. Some enemies can only be hit by ranged attacks, and sometimes you need to block or parry to take an enemy down, making a lot of stuff satisfying to do if you manage to pull it off properly. Bosses too have their own mechanics that, despite some of them being somewhat difficult, are pretty easy to understand.

While the story follows the traditional RPG trend of clearing dungeons to unlock new areas and cities, there is a lot more to it than that, something that is quite clear from the beginning. The way the story is told leaves you with a lot of questions in the beginning that will slowly be answered the more you play, making it easier to take it in and process. Something I'm gonna address immediately is that, despite Lea (the main character) not talking much and with limited words, her personality shines through incredibly well. Her expressions and actions are enough to make clear what she is like. That is not to say the other characters aren't as good though, however Lea is a great example on how to do a (mostly) silent character right.

The graphics are a very well done pixel style. Despite the limited pixels, the game is very detailed and animations feel fluid. The animations of the special skills look pretty good as well, but never too overwhelming. I have however gotten some graphical errors, but I feel like that's more on me. However it could also be because of the engine it uses. The music on the other hand is straight up perfect to me. The battle music is nice to listen to, some character themes (especially Apollo's theme) are great, and the overworld music tends to be very relaxed.

While I had some frustrations with this game, that was mainly me lacking patience. Overall I very much enjoyed the game and I feel horrible for leaving my friend waiting for so long to play this game. Oh, yeah did I tell you this game is made in javascript? Yeah, the demo is still playable in a browser to this date!
