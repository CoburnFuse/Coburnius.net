[ Return to games I like ]

“I remember everything and it changes nothing!”

Dust was pretty much my proper introduction to metroidvania games. I am fairly sure I have played some before that, but never to a good extent and likely only gave it a single try too. Well that sure went well, as for the longest of times, I did consider this my favorite game, which was more or less like ten years or so.

I can't exactly compare it fairly to other games in the genre, so I am bound to make some remarks that may or may not make sense. The game boils down to exploring areas, jumping from ledge to ledge, finding treasures here and there and defeating enemies, using various skills you obtain during the journey. Enemies have different ways of defeating. Some of them are plain and easy, while others require a more precise approach like stunning them via a parry (which are incredibly satisfying to pull off!) What surprised me is how incredibly responsive the game is. If a mistake was made, it was because I made that mistake, and not because the game was pulling something weird or bugging out or anything. Combat feels rewarding and there are always reasons to come back to previous areas whenever you unlock a new ability or learn something new that could be used somewhere else to find secret treasures.

The game does start off in, what I believe is the past or something like that, giving you a combat tutorial. However, while it may be a weird way to approach it, the way it's done makes complete sense once you progress through the story. Everything in the story feels very personal to Dust (the main character) which makes it all the more interesting. Dust himself is also very interesting. Having this mysterious past and goal, which all will be explained in time the more you progress the story. And then there is Fidget. She is... annoying in a way. However, she is also incredibly likable and goofy, and I have grown to like her quite a bit, even have a shirt with her on it. There is also Ahrah, the blade that Dust is wielding, being a voice of reason and a moral guide. The three together make such an incredible dynamic, each complementing the others greatly.

A few looks at just screenshots of the game will show you how gorgeous the game is. Everything is hand-painted and it shows. There is so much love and care put into this part alone. Whether it's the colorful forest, the haunted plains, or the dark caves. All of these places look beautiful one way or another. The music, while not as special as the graphics (which mind you is not meant in a bad way), are still fantastic and just fits the areas and battles just right.

While I can't say I've grown fond of the genre, Dust did make me want to try other games of this genre. And while it's not my favorite game of all-time anymore, I am pretty sure it will never leave my top 5 ever. Oh right, did I mention that aside from the music and voice acting, nearly everything is created by a single person?
