“Don't hesitate. I'll be fine!”

I think we're all familiar with Snake. You know, that simple game where you collect dots to increase the length of the snake you are controlling. Now, what if we add some more chaos to it by combining it with a bullet hell? Yeah, that is what Last Command is. While the concept sounds very wild just on paper, it works out incredibly well.

As mentioned in the intro, this game is pretty much a combination of Snake and any bullet hell. You collect parts, and you shoot those parts at the boss. Each boss does actually feel quite unique, aside from the tutorial ones, with each of them stuff you need to figure out from the cues that are shown. I never had the feeling any boss was particularly unfair, unless I just could not figure out what was going on. While I did originally play it on a lower difficulty, the game does shine a lot more on higher difficulties. I've gotten to appreciate harder games like that quite a lot more, even though I have gotten really frustrated at times. Though, that's likely just a typical skill issue more than anything. You'll also obtain a bunch of passive and active skills that you can apply in battles, which is always a trial and error until you find something that matches your style.

The story does actually start with the prologue (which doubles as the demo.) where the server you start in is getting deleted. You learn the controls here, as well as the basics of battles. That being said though, this will also be taught in the full game. You will quickly figure out that there's a lot of weird stuff going on, even at the start of the game. There are a lot of very dark twists and turns that, in hindsight, I should have known a lot earlier than the reveal. When it comes to the characters, I've grown fond of Fei (a character you meet very early on) really quickly, she is kind but also goofy. There are a bunch of characters you meet at some other point, but I'd leave it up to you to discover all of that, if you decide to play the game.

The graphics have a good mix between modern and retro. The generally has a more 3D look to it, similar to overworld maps in older RPGs. The battles have a big focus on the pixel-art style, which is very fitting considering the theme of the game. There are a good set of options regarding color-blindness in the options if some colors are difficult to see in battle as well, making it a lot more accessible. The music is also just great, every boss (aside from minor battles) has their own theme (or variation of one.) But somehow aren't too distracting despite the intensity of some of them.

If you aren't entirely sure if you want to play the game, just play the prologue/demo and see if it's something you like. If you like the demo, you will absolutely like the full game as well!
