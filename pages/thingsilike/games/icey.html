[ Return to games I like ]

“In the middle of a dark, quiet room filled only by the sounds of humming machines, a figure floats, suspended.”

While it's been a long time since I was introduced to this game, which I think was via a video review of it, I sure as hell don't regret buying and playing the game. I'm also fairly sure this was the first game I had ever fully completed (despite it not being that big of a feat.)

The game is a fairly simple to learn but somewhat difficult to master hack-and-slash game. You follow the narrator (or not) while defeating enemies. The combat is pretty fast and tends to be quite unforgiving, something that I always have mixed feelings about, but generally favor over hand-holding. Pulling off combos on a boss or being able to defeat one without taking damage is very satisfying. While the game is somewhat linear, there are a lot of side paths for you to explore, leading to areas that feel completely out of place, yet in context make a lot of sense. A part of the game also plays outside of the actual game in a fake computer, adding to the entire meta feeling the game has.

The story is more or less fully narrative driven and the entire goal is to find out what is going on with ICEY. There is a lot of meta humor, especially when you aren't following the narrator's story. The narrator reacts to pretty much everything you do, whether it is following the story, messing around by waiting or repeatedly falling off a cliff (which leads to great dialogue), or straight up going against the story. The game is also short enough to be able to completely do in a (long) single sitting. The main menu also is very helpful with guiding you through the game to get the perfect ending as well, leaving you with little vague guessing.

The game looks incredibly appealing. The futuristic style of the game is very much my style, especially combined with its bleak yet vibrant color palette. Every area does feel completely different even though it's still all very closely together. While the animations look a bit robotic and with a “low framerate” (for lack of better words), it very much adds to the artstyle of the game and looks very fluid. While there isn't too much music in the game, the music that is in the game is great, with some standing out a lot like the Clock Tower. Oh, also, the OST is completely free to download, so there is that.

Unfortunately, it has been a while, so my memories of this game are somewhat vague, though that should be a good reason to play this game again. Like I said, it's a game that is perfect for a single long sitting!
