[ Return to games I like ]

"You have all this power, and you're not gonna use it to fight evil?"

I've always had a preference for the more easy-going games when it comes to platformers, which typically are the 3D ones, if they are done well of course. A Hat in Time fits that one for me perfectly. It's cute, not too difficult, and doesn't rely on too much gimmicky stuff that I'm typically not a fan of.

Naturally, the entire goal is to obtain the collectibles scattered around stages, which in this case are hourglasses called Time Pieces. Though there are a bunch of other collectibles to collect as well that will unlock a bunch of new abilities and bonus stages. Jumping from platform to platform, exploring the different stages to find the collectibles, and defeating enemies and bosses. The stages are very diverse and all of them feel very different while still feeling like they belong in the game. The first stage feels like a very typical 3D-platformer stage, while the second stage feels a lot more linear and story driven. While the game isn't too difficult, it still feels very rewarding and satifying to finally get that collectible or to defeat a boss.

While the overarching story is to obtain the Time Pieces, every stage has its own story as well which are separate from the main story. Though in the end, it all is connected one way or another. Though it is a platformer first, so the story is very simple and that is completely fine. What does stand out is the characters you meet in the game. Whether it's Mustache Girl who you meet in the very first stage who has a vendetta against the mafia, the birds from the second stage who have this intense rivalry, or the clearly evil Snatcher who is pretty much the incarnation of the devil, forcing you to do the stuff he wants you to do. All of these characters have their own stories that you discover as you play the game to completion.

It's very clearly a super cutesy looking game. The colors are vibrant, even in the murkiest of levels, and it is very pleasing on the eyes. I've been noticing a lot of details the more I've been playing the game. It's clear they put quite some effort into making the worlds. The music is also fantastic. Each stage has variations on the music depending on the level and situation, and each boss has their own great battle theme, making sure you get pumped up for the battle.

If you're looking for a 3D-platformer, this is the one you will need. It's not overly long and has a lot of variation to it. And if you need some more content, there are a few pretty good DLCs, with one of them adding insanely difficult variations of stages, as well as a community making a lot of custom stages for you to try!
