“That is right. Welcome to Homestedt!”

While I wasn't part of the people that was waiting, for the longest of times, there were a few loose ends that were never addressed and Lea was more or less stuck in some form of limbo that would be something like in-between chapters. However, the wait feels very much worth it considering this DLC is amazing.

It takes place a few months after the main game when some things have settled after the hectic events of the main game. T he MMO has developed further and the final dungeon is about to be released. However, there are still some things to do. You get a place to live which serves as a bit of a hub and you can fully explore the big city (Rhombus Square) as well as a new smaller dungeon before the final dungeon. There is a bunch of new equipment you can get (and which you should get.) While I originally had some gripes with the puzzles of the main game, the puzzles in the final dungeon I had no issue with. The boss of the dungeon also was very fun and had some genuine difficulty to it, making it a good challenge.

While the gameplay is practically the same, they did improve on the mechanics of dungeons and bosses. The areas fit right in with the rest of the game graphically, and the boss of the final dungeon has some great animations. The few new pieces of music that have been added also fit right in the rest of the game.

While the DLC is a lot shorter than the main game, it's still absolutely worth it. The new content is a great addition and it's great to get some closure on the story. Just get the DLC if you have finished the main story!
