“I am a collector - of many things; art and artifacts, treasures and truths…and curios that occupy the periphery of desire.”

I find there is a lot of serenity in doom and destruction, as weird as it sounds. The eldritch nature of this game, despite all of its horrors, puts me at ease for some reason. Well, as long as I am not chased down by whatever is underwater of course.

The entire goal of the game is just fishing and dredging up parts to upgrade your boat. There's different areas all with different kinds of fish, and requiring different tools to catch depending on the area or water. Eventually though, you'll be able to get new tools that work in more than a single type of water, so it's always very much worth it to explore and find the stuff to upgrade your tools. The gameplay loop is more or less just, catch fish, find parts, sell stuff, and... don't go insane from all the stuff that is happening. While survival is not the hardest part, you still need to survive and making careless mistakes will cost you, especially in the open waters with all unknown creatures out and about. Despite the very simple gameplay loop, it still feels very engaging and relaxed, even when exploring.

It's very difficult to talk about the story without giving too many spoilers, however, when starting out, you will very quickly realize a lot of things happening that feel wrong. People behave strangely, the fish in the waters look eldritch and corrupt without rhyme or reason, and the tasks you are given feel odd and specific. This is genuinely as much as I can tell you without going way in depth, considering it's a game you really need to experience yourself, and it's best to just know as little as possible, finding out everything yourself.

Graphically, the game looks murky and grim, however, that's a massive compliment for this game, as it captures the feeling of the game perfectly. Bright colors would not work for this game at all, and yet, the game still feels vibrant in its own way. The artstyle of the game is very stylized and it further enhances the eldritch feeling of this game. There are some great pieces of music as well, a notable one being “The Restless Town” you hear in the main villages where you start. The music in Dredge gives this feeling of uneasiness and dread, but it also feels oddly relaxing in a way, especially combined with the ambience of the game.

Dredge is a terrifying game, but it's also beautiful. Despite the looming darkness and threats everywhere, taking your time to take it all in is the best way to play it. In the end, its still just a completely normal fishing experience!
