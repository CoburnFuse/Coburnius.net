“As pieces of the shredded starship fall around you, you start making plans to survive.”

It's quite hard to describe this game as a game. It doesn't exactly feel like one. I think the best way to describe it is an interactive story simulator of some sorts. However, there is so much more to it than just that still.

The game gives you quite a lot of freedom on what to do. You can pick how to start, how hard you want it all to be, what pawns (the name for the characters) you want to start with, etc. When in-game, it's already quite the sandbox, albeit it's generally the best decision to just make sure you can survive the first few hours. Plan buildings, plot farms and mining zones, etc. All part of needing to get resources and survive the hell that awaits you, considering everything can go down fast if you aren't prepared for it. Though all of that is things you need to learn. The game also has an incredibly massive modding community, so if you want to add something to your game that isn't normally in there, there's a big chance there is a mod for it.

After you properly settle, just do whatever you want with your colony. If you want to be some peaceful farmers, go ahead. If you want to be aggressive raiders, you can absolutely do that. The game has the reputation for being a war crime simulator after all, considering you can do a lot of things that would never be acceptable. Personally, I think the best way to play is to do it in some roleplay-ish kind of fashion, thinking about what fits your colony and building a story around that. The Ideology expansion very much expands on that idea of that, allowing for an even more story-telling way of playing. But then again, there is no wrong way to play it, as long as you're having fun with the game. The game is a sandbox after all.

While graphically not that impressive, it fits the kind of game perfectly. It is simplistic in nature and helps keep it from being too cluttered, since a lot can happen at once during times. That being said though, the UI can feel very messy, especially for new players and this is quite the learning curve you need to get through to understand the game more. The music is generally very relaxing and feels more like an ambience more than anything, however, it's still something nice to listen to in the background as it is pretty relaxing music.

If you are into sandbox-y games that give you a lot of freedom, and are also punishing (if you want it to be) this might just be the game for you. You can tweak the game a lot with the massive amount of mods available, to the point where you might not even recognize what is vanilla and what is not anymore. Honestly, you should genuinely just experience this game for yourself!
