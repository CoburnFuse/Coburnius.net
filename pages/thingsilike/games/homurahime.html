[ Return to games I like ]

“Don't forget your purpose. You exist to eradicate demons from the world.”

I found out about this game a long time before it was released while scrolling through the game news site I always visit. The idea of a hack and slash with bullet hell elements very much intrigued me so I was making sure to keep myself up to date with news regarding the game. When the demo was out, it felt a bit rough but overall I really enjoyed it.

Hack and slash kind of games always look pretty simple, but generally there is a lot more going on. Homura Hime is absolutely no exception to this. You need to make sure to respond to all possible threats at any time, which can be very chaotic at times, but always ends up being quite rewarding when pulling it all off. Combat feels very fluid, pulling off successful parry's is very satisfying especially during deadman's volley-esque part, and just throwing out awesome looking special moves is great. Aside from combat, there is also an exploration part to this game which also involves platforming. While the platforming can be a little bit frustrating, it doesn't involve a lot of the game, and is generally quite forgiving (unlike the combat.) The boss fights especially can be quite unforgiving, but that does make it all the more satisfying once you beat them.

The game revolves around eliminating demonic presences. Of course, this is a very simplified way of saying it, but that is still basically what it is about. You learn their stories, and you learn more about the world as you progress the game. While there are a lot more characters than just the two protagonists, the story does mainly revolve around Ann and Homura Hime. Ann is quite the cheerful yet somewhat ditzy girl, and Homura the more stoic and “professional” one. However, the dynamic between the two works incredibly well, and the moments they have together outside of combat are quite wholesome.

It's a very vibrant game, even during the darker areas, which appeals very much to me. Yet, despite the vibrancy, they made it very easy to read the tells of enemies, and the environment isn't at all distracting during combat. The areas all have a very distinct feeling to them, so every area feels completely new which is very nice. Just like with most games I play, I really enjoy the music, especially the boss themes. They aren't distracting and tend to give you an idea about what the character is, and the sense of urgency of the situation at hand... wait, why does this boss have eurobeat!? (which yes, it completely fits the boss you're fighting!)

I was very glad I just sort of stumbled upon this game. While it took a little bit to come out, and while the demo was a bit rough, I have absolutely zero regrets playing this game. Everything just feels quite right, even the length of the game, making it a good game to replay!
