[ Return to games I like ]

“I wish I were something that I truly wanted to be. Not what someone else tells me I want to be.”

It's not exactly a secret that I generally like Touhou. It's also not exactly a secret that I like TTRPG stuff, especially considering I have an entire page dedicated to my old DND characters. So when I saw that a developer mixed these two together, I figured why not give it a shot. While the initial demo didn't feel quite right, the updated demo convinced me to purchase it, so I did.

The entire game revolves around rolling dice, like a typical TTRPG game would. In this case, you only roll six-sided dice. However, unlike traditional six-sided dice, there are a lot of different variants of these. There are ones that go from two through seven, ones where half of them are zero, etc. So while obviously it's all still luck based, there is some strategizing involved. You would think that higher is always better, but they give you the possible outcomes beforehand, and then you will notice that sometimes you want to roll high, and sometimes you want to roll low. So having a variety of dice is a must here. And if you don't like the roll you made, you can always try to “cheat” your way through, which, considering Reimu, is not the weirdest thing she would do. During the game, you'll also have to make decisions that are suggested by the residents of the Scarlet Devil mansion, all of them fitting for who they are.

The story is viewed from the perspective of the residents of the Scarlet Devil mansion, who are playing a game revolving around Reimu who is seeking out Marisa. What you'll quickly notice, and what I am very pleasantly surprised by, is that all narration during the gameplay is voiced. Unfortunately, the playing residents are not voiced, though that didn't really take away the enjoyment I have. They very clearly went for this “game master” kind of narration here and it really makes it a lot more exciting as if you are actually playing a TTRPG game.

The graphics make it even more clear that it's taken up the inspiration from traditional TTRPG games. You move pieces around on a tiled board, Reimu and Marisa are actual mini's that one would use during TTRPG games, and all others are cardboard cutouts, something that people would also typically do for NPCs. All of this gives it a very authentic feel to it and I very much appreciate that. As expected of a Touhou game, fan game or not, the music is great. Every battle and area has their own style to it, and I'm very fond of the first and the last boss theme in the game, the latter I am not going to share because of spoilers.

While there are some very frustrating parts, considering it is all still based around RNG, in the end, I enjoyed this game a lot more than I expected. It's extremely story driven, there is humor, and there is action (that you just have to visualize for yourself, considering it's a TTRPG game!)
