[ Return to anime I like ]

“Guriddo biiiiiiiimu!”

If you are into tokusatsu, the name “Gridman” might sound familiar to you. Because this is adaptation/sequel-ish of Gridman the Hyper Agent. While I'm not too familiar with that entire genre myself, this is still a really good watch and I'm really glad a friend of mine recommended it to me, even if it took a while for me to get to it, which after watching, I should't have waited so long.

Something that really surprised me is that everything has a reason for existing or happening, nothing feels like it's done for the sake of just doing it. And while it does follow a more typical “monster of the week” kind of story, it doesn't drag down the overarching story at all. There are a lot of small details that are missable with the characters as well, albeit a lot of things I can't really tell without spoiling big parts of the story. That being said though, the ending was also incredibly well executed, and a great twist to the story. The characters are also a colorful bunch, whether it's the protagonist team or the antagonist team, they are all likeable (or made to not be liked at all, which is a positive thing in this case!) and well thought out.

It's not a surprise that it looks visually stunning, considering the studio that worked on it is TRIGGER. Can't say much about it aside from it being really good, vibrant, and appealing, it's as simple as that. Albeit, the appealing visuals are much more shown during the battles as there is a lot more happening there. The music is also great, even having some good throwbacks to the original series, showing that the people working on this anime really cared for what they were making.

This is something I can straight up recommend to anyone (unless you're really not into action, which in that case, fair enough.) It's not too long, the story is great, so on and so on. If you like this one though, you might want to check out the sequel SSSS.Dynazenon, as well as the movie (in order) as well!
