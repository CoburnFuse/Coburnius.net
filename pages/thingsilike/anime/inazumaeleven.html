Let me just get this out of the way, I don't really like football at all. I find it boring and to me it's just a bunch of people running after a ball and pretending to get hurt in the most dramatic way possible. However, I do really like over the top things, and somehow, Inazuma Eleven, despite it being football. Somehow, even being one of (if not my most) favorite anime at the time of writing.

While the story is a bit simple and typical, it's the cast and the growth of said cast that do it for me. That being said though, there are some seriously sinister and dark points in the story, especially when the antagonists are involved, and something that may genuinely spook some younger people. As for the cast, Raimon (the protagonist team) has characters each with pretty distinct personalities, which are also reflected in the moves they are using. For example, Gouenji is a fiery and very passionate player and has all these moves based around fire. And Kabeyama is a rather bulky looking player who really wants to protect his team, with his moves being based around mountains. Each team they are fighting against also has a very clear theme going on and of course, moves based around that.

While we're at the topic of the special moves though, this is what drew me in originally. They look great and (depending on the move) vibrant and pretty (or dark.) Every time a new move was shown, I would get pretty excited just by the visuals alone, especially if they are more over the top like in later seasons. The music is also just great, matching the vibe of what's going on on screen. Whenever the opening plays, or any vocal track for that matter, you know something cool is going to happen. Because of that, I may or may not have caught myself cheering along a bit too enthusiastically at times, but you know what, it's football right, so nothing wrong with that, right?

It is quite a long anime, so even just watching half of the first season (which is 13 episodes) should give you a very good idea of what it is like. Though, later seasons get quite wild as well. If you don't like football, don't use that as a reason not to watch it, it's absolutely still worth a shot. I didn't think it would be one of my favorites at that time, but here I am, having watched all of it multiple times!
